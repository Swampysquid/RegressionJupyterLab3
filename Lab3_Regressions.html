Statistical Linear Regression

Scatterplots

Scatterplots are a way to show multiple points of data, on a case-by-case basis, when data is contingent on 2 variables. The Scatterplots in this project were contingent on Temperature v. Height, Rain v. Height, Altitude v. Height, and Site v. Height. The Scatterplots have blue dots representing a data point, with height always being the Y-axis and the other variable being on the X-Axis.

Line of Best Fit

The Line of Best Fit is applied to the Scatterplots. This line is the line of least variation from the points, therefore it fits the points best. It is represented on the Scatterplots as a red line going through the points.

Standard Error

The Standard Error is the approximate standard deviation of a sample of a population; it finds the difference between the values and their mean, as a summation. It will be used in order to find the Coefficient of Determination.

R Squared

The R Squared is the Coefficient of Determination. This is a measure of the amount of variation in the dependent variable(height), which can be explained by the independent variable. Essentially, the R2 will see how strong of a correlation there is (if any) with the line of best fit compared to the actual values. Based on the R Squared Values below, rain would have the strongest correlation to height of the four independent variables listed.
